Publications

  • 2025.09.01
    CHaRNet: Conditioned Heatmap Regression for Robust Dental Landmark Localization
    Arxiv
    Introduced CHaRNet, the first fully end-to-end deep learning framework for anatomical landmark detection in 3D dental models. CHaRNet operates directly on point clouds, bypassing tooth segmentation, and uses a novel Conditioned Heatmap Regression module to robustly handle missing teeth, achieving high accuracy and streamlining orthodontic workflows.
  • 2025.05.01
    Land use and land cover fraction estimation for Sentinel-2 RGB images: A new LULC mapping task
    Remote Sensing Applications: Society and Environment
    Proposed a novel LULC fraction estimation task for public Sentinel-2 RGB images, predicting both the presence and fractional abundance of LULC classes. We introduced Land-1.0, the first open-source dataset of its kind, and developed multitask deep learning models that offer a scalable, cost-effective solution for environmental monitoring.
  • 2024.01.01
    Bidirectional recurrent imputation and abundance estimation of LULC classes with MODIS multispectral time series and geo-topographic and climatic data
    IEEE Journal of Selected Topics in Applied Earth Observations and Remote Sensing
    We present a novel deep learning approach for spectral unmixing of MODIS multispectral time series that addresses missing data through end-to-end bidirectional LSTM models. Our method integrates spectral-temporal data with geo-topographic and climatic information to estimate LULC abundances in mixed pixels without explicit endmember extraction. We created the Andalusia MultiSpectral MultiTemporal Unmixing dataset with monthly MODIS data at 460-m resolution, demonstrating significant improvements in LULC abundance estimation.
  • 2021.11.18
    CI-dataset and DetDSCI methodology for detecting too small and too large critical infrastructures in satellite images: airports and electrical substations as case study
    IEEE Journal of Selected Topics in Applied Earth Observations and Remote Sensing
    Proposed the CI-dataset and a novel DetDSCI methodology to detect critical infrastructures of varying scales, such as airports and electrical substations, in satellite imagery. The two-level, resolution-independent approach significantly improves detection, achieving up to a 37.53% F1 score improvement over standard models like Faster R-CNN.
